Why SMEC ?



Neemrana evokes memories of bygone eras, where the Aravali range meets the plains of Haryana, ruled by a dynasty of Chauhan Kings under the benign surroundings of lush greenery and protective, steep slopes of hillocks. The Neemrana Fort Palace is unique in design and structure. After centuries of neglect of these areas under foreign rule, the slumber is changing into motivated activity.




The national and international tourists, statesmen requiring undisturbed peace for their serious deliberations and of course creative artists are veering towards the Neemrana Fort Palace day in and day out. On weekends there is hardly any vacant suite in the fort converted hotel.

The decision of Govt. of Rajasthan to build an Industrial Complex in the foothills has transformed life of this region. National Highway bristle with traffic on 24 X 7 basis. The restaurants and food joints flourish on both sides. The entrepreneurs from all states have gravitated towards this area to establish their industries be it bakeries or breweries, glass or synthetics.

Amidst this fertile land has emerged an Institutional Area. To meet the global demand of manpower in the field of Information Technology, Telecommunications and Management, a number of educational institutions have pitched their tents. The state and central governments, public sector banks & undertakings and foremost of all the philanthropic private education societies are vying with each other to open the institutes of education and training in these fields. 

St. Margaret Engineering College is drawing students, not only from its surroundings, but also from metropolis of Delhi & Jaipur. The easy accessibility area, because of Delhi - Jaipur Highway (NH 8) , has added special attraction for parents and students of even far flung towns. Today, Neemrana is micro-cosmic. The managers in the industry and educationists in the institutes are creating a unique progressive culture. The young population of students and trainees are exposed to the global environment in their daily lives through right preparation and training for future global careers. 

The Government of Rajasthan has rightly felt the pulse of these activities and selected Neemrana as a future world-class city in the National Capital Region. Neemrana is the knowledge city of the coming days. Information Technology will be the hub of this knowledge and an educational activity will be the foremost enterprise.

Again, Institute of vision and reach, St. Margaret Engineering College is going to show the way of all-round progress of knowledge and information. The Information Industry of Gurgaon is half-planned extension of Delhi. Neemrana will be fully planned, a much more modern and of course a city which will be the envy of the most beautiful cities of the world.

Those studying in its institutes and serving in its industries will remember themselves as proud pioneers of Neemrana – a city on the world map.
